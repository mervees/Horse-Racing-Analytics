# Assumptions, Limitations & Honest Caveats

This project is a **research and learning framework** for horse-racing
modelling. It is engineered to be correct and extensible. It is **not** a
money-making system, and nothing here should be read as a prediction that it
will be profitable. Please read this page before drawing any conclusion from
the numbers.

## The shipped numbers are illustrative, not evidence of profit

Unless you have placed the real Kaggle CSVs in `data/raw/`, the pipeline runs
on **synthetic data** generated by `src/data/synthetic.py`. That generator
deliberately injects a learnable signal and a slightly noisy market so the
machinery has something to find. As a result:

- Any positive ROI you see on synthetic data is a property of the simulator,
  **not** a forecast of real-world returns.
- The synthetic market was tuned to be *fairly* efficient, but it is still far
  simpler and more beatable than a real pari-mutuel pool.

The honest purpose of the synthetic numbers is to show that the **scoring,
de-vigging, calibration, and backtesting code works end to end** — nothing more.

## Real betting markets are highly efficient

The HK win pool is one of the most liquid, sharply priced betting markets in
the world. Two hard truths follow:

1. **The takeout is large.** Win-pool takeout is roughly **17–22%**. Every
   dollar bet pays that to the operator before any skill is rewarded. A bettor
   who simply backs favourites should expect to lose approximately the takeout
   over time — and in this lab the `market_favourite` baseline is included
   precisely so you can see that floor.
2. **Beating the takeout is extremely hard.** To profit you must be more
   accurate than the aggregate of every other bettor *by more than the
   margin*. Most models that look profitable in backtest are exhibiting
   leakage, overfitting, or unrealistic price assumptions rather than genuine
   edge.

## Specific modelling limitations

- **Final odds, not live odds.** The dataset has settled/final win and place
  odds. A real betting operation must act on prices available *before* the off;
  the price you can actually get (and closing-line value) is not modelled here.
- **No market microstructure.** Bet timing, pool movement, minimum bets,
  rebates/commission, and liquidity are not simulated. The strategy ROI ignores
  these frictions.
- **Win market only.** Place/quinella/exotic markets, dead-heats, and
  scratchings/non-runners are not modelled.
- **Limited feature surface.** No external weather feed (track *going* is the
  proxy), no detailed pace/sectional pre-race modelling, no
  trainer–jockey-combo or course-and-distance specialisation yet. See
  "Improvement ideas" in [MODEL_DESIGN.md](MODEL_DESIGN.md).
- **Single chronological split.** One 70/30 time split, not walk-forward
  evaluation; ROI confidence intervals are not yet computed. At realistic
  sample sizes, ROI estimates are very noisy.
- **Calibration is not yet enforced.** Probabilities are normalised within a
  race but not isotonic/Platt-calibrated on held-out data.

## Leakage: guarded, but verify on real data

The most dangerous failure mode in racing models is **data leakage** — letting
post-race or same-race information into the features. The lab guards against it
with prior-only ("cumulative-minus-current", "shift-then-roll") features, a
strict pre-race/result column split in `schema.py`, and a chronological
backtest split. `tests/test_leakage.py` asserts these properties. **When you
attach real data and add new features, re-run those tests and add new ones** —
leakage tends to sneak in through innocent-looking joins and aggregates.

## Statistical caution

- Past performance does not predict future results — in racing this is
  literally true and not a disclaimer cliché.
- A few hundred bets cannot distinguish skill from variance. Treat single-split
  ROI as a rough diagnostic, not proof.
- Multiple-testing risk is real: try enough strategies and one will look good by
  chance. Pre-register what you will test.

## Responsible use

Betting involves a real risk of financial loss, and for some people gambling
becomes harmful. This software is provided for **education and research**. Do
not bet money you cannot afford to lose. If gambling is affecting you or someone
you know, please reach out to a local support service (for example, in many
countries a national gambling helpline is available). Never treat any output of
this tool as a guaranteed or recommended bet.

## What this project *is* good for

- Learning how scoring, fair-odds/de-vigging, Kelly sizing, calibration, and
  time-aware backtesting fit together in one clean, tested codebase.
- A leakage-safe scaffold you can point at real data and extend responsibly.
- Generating plain-language, fact-grounded explanations of model output.
